The single exit price (SEP) mechanism in South Africa specifies the maximum price that a medicine can be charged at. Dispensers may charge an additional dispensing fee depending on the price of the medicine. The Medicines and Related Substances Act allows for the following charges:

  • Where the SEP is less than R81.00, the maximum dispensing fee is R6.30 + 46% of the SEP.
  • Where the SEP is less than R216.00, the maximum dispensing fee is R16.00 + 33% of the SEP.
  • Where the SEP is less than R756.00, the maximum dispensing fee is R52.00 + 15% of the SEP.
  • Where the SEP is greater than or equal to R756.00, the maximum dispensing fee is R123.00 + 5% of the SEP.

This prices listed here include the maximum allowed dispensing fee. Charging more than this amount is a contravention of the Mediciens and Related Substances Act.
